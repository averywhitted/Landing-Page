The Audition Survival Workshop

This workshop covers what actually moves the needle from submission to callback, with tools you can use immediately.

Workshops are held on Zoom and are currently limited to 10 people.

  • How to find better auditions through Actors Access, Backstage, EPAs, and reps.
  • Prep systems that stick: memorization methods, line drills, and fast scene choices.
  • Self-tape workflow: reader setup, lighting, edits, file delivery, and submission habits.
  • In-room and callback strategy: handling adjustments, nerves, and post-audition reset.
  • What you leave with: a repeatable framework, tools for handling anxiety, and live feedback on your work.

What to Expect

The workshop runs two hours on Zoom with a maximum of ten actors. Each session follows a clear structure — we move through the full audition cycle from finding opportunities to leaving the room — but there's time built in for your specific questions and live feedback on your work.

This isn't a lecture. Expect to participate. Part of each session is spent working on material in real time, with direct feedback on your choices, your habits, and the specific places you tend to get stuck.

You walk out with a repeatable framework and a clearer read on where your actual sticking points are — not a set of notes to revisit later.

  • Format Group workshop on Zoom
  • Duration Two hours
  • Group size Max 10 actors
  • What to bring Yourself — and any current sides if you have them
  • What you leave with A repeatable framework and live feedback on your work

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before booking.

Is the workshop held on Zoom?
Yes. All sessions are remote via Zoom. You'll receive a link after booking.
How many people are in each session?
Sessions are capped at ten actors to keep the feedback specific and the room manageable.
Is this for beginners or working actors?
Both. The workshop is built for actors who are actively auditioning or working toward it — whether you're just starting out or have been at it for years, the tools are immediately usable.
How is the workshop different from private coaching?
The workshop covers the full audition cycle in a group format. Private coaching is one-on-one and goes deeper on whatever you're specifically working through — a particular audition, a recurring problem, or ongoing prep support.
What should I have ready?
Just yourself. Bring any current sides if you have them, but nothing is required in advance.
How do I find out about upcoming dates?
Use the email signup and you'll hear directly when new workshop dates are announced.
